Sara A. Stoudt

My name is Sara Stoudt. I am a fourth year student in the UC Berkeley Statistics Department and a Berkeley Institute for Data Science Fellow. Prior to being a BIDS Fellow, I was supported by a National Physical Sciences Consortium Fellowship with the National Institute of Standards and Technology and part of the Data Science for the 21st Century: Environment and Society Training Program . My research focus is on ecological applications of statistics, and I am advised by Will Fithian and Perry de Valpine. We are currently working on assessing the identifiability and robustness of inference under model misspecification in species distribution models. I am also involved in teaching writing for statistics with Professor Deborah Nolan. Previously, I received a B.A. in Mathematics from Smith College with an emphasis on Statistics . Ask me about DataFest or Statsbites.
